Plant Care Tips

Choosing the right size pot for your plant

Choosing the right size pot for your plants can be a challenging task. However there are some simple ways to determine what size pot is best. First measure the length across the top of the pot, from one of the circle to the other. Then using this measurement you generally want to add 1-3 inches depending on the type of plant. Plants with large root system prefer a larger pot, while shallow root plants will prefer a smaller pot.

How to control pest

There are many forms of pest control, from irritants to pesticide. A simple non-chemical approach to warding off pest such as aphids, ants, or slugs is diatomaceous earth. This course powder damages these insects, helping to ward them off of your plants, while also avoiding harsh chemicals. Some common irritants are certain oils, soaps, and baking soda can help to control other pest that diatomaceous earth won't work on. As a last resort there are the chemical pesticides, although typically effective, improper handling can be hazardous.
